Introduction

Bihar, located in the eastern part of India, is a state rich in cultural heritage and intellectual history. Derived from the Sanskrit word 'Vihara' (Buddhist monastery), Bihar has been the center of power and learning for millennia.

Founded: March 22, 1912 (Separated from Bengal Presidency).

Capital: Patna (Ancient Pataliputra).

Primary Source of Income

Agriculture is the backbone of Bihar's economy. The state is a leading producer of Litchi, Makhana (Fox Nuts), and Vegetables. Key industries include Food Processing, Sugarcane, and Textiles (Bhagalpuri Silk).
